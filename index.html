A waterfall is a natural feature where water flows over a vertical drop or a series of steep drops in the course of a river or stream. Waterfalls typically form in the upper course of a river where the flow of water is strong and the landscape is more rugged. They occur when a river meets a sudden change in elevation, often due to erosion-resistant rock overlaying softer rock beneath.
